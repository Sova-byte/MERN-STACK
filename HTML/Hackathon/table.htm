üåê The Basics: Why Tables Are Data Superheroes
Tables turn chaotic numbers/lists into organized grids‚Äîlike spreadsheets on your webpage. Perfect for schedules, comparisons, or menus. The table tag family creates rows and cells‚Äîstructure first, style later with CSS! Use them wisely (not for layout‚ÄîCSS Grid wins there). üöÄ

üìã Table Tag: table tag (The Grid Container)
  • What It Does: Wraps your entire table-hold rows, headers, and data.
  • Structure: opens, then fill with tr tag (tr) (rows)inside.
  • Key Helpers:
    • thead: Header section (top row, bold by default)
    • tbody: Body section (main data)
    • tfoot: Footer section (totals, notes)

üìè Rows & Cells: tr & td (The Grid Bricks)
  • tr (Table Row): Creates one horizontal line-holds cells across
  • td (Table Data): Individual cells-put your content here (text, images, links).
  • Structure: Each tr needs same number of td or th -tables hate mismatches!

üîó Merging Cells: colspan & rowspan (The Spanners)
  • colspan="n": Cell spans n columns wide (e.g., colspan="2" = double width).
  • rowspan="n": Cell spans n rows tall (e.g., rowspan="3" = triple height).
  • Use Cases: Headers, merged categories, or irregular layouts.
