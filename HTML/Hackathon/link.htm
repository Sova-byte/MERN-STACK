Links turn static pages into a web‚Äîliterally! They let users jump between your pages, explore other sites, or dive into resources. The a tag is your portal gun: point it anywhere with href (hyper reference). Master this, and your site becomes navigable! üöÄ


üìÑ Creating Links Between Your Pages (Internal Links)
  • What It Does: Connects different pages on your site-like Home->About->Contact.
  • Structure: <a href="filename.html">Link Text</a>
  • Key Rules:
    • Use relative pathsfor same sites (no full URL).
    • Same folder: href="about.html"
    • Subfolder: href="pages/contact.html"
    • Parent folder: href="../index.html" (up one level)
üåç Linking to Other Sites (External Links)
  • What It Does: Jumps to website outside yours-like linking to Google or a tutorial.
  • Structure <a href="https://full-url.com">Link Text</a> (always use full HTTPS URL)
  • Best Practices:
    • Target="_blank": Opens in new tab(don't trap users!).
üéØ Link Types & Special Destinations
  • To Email: <aa href="mailto:hello@example.com">Email Me</a> (opens email client).
  • To Phone: <a href="tel: +917854691548">Call Me</a> (dials on mobile)
  • To File Download: <a href="resume.pdf" download>Download Resume</a>(forces save)
